### Preferred term label: Abnormal fetal thoracic circumference [Ready to SUBMIT]

**Synonyms:** Abnormal fetal TC; decreased TC

**Definition:** An abnormal (reduced) fetal thoracic circumference is defined as a measurement below the 2.5th or 5th percentile for gestational age. The thoracic circumference is assessed on a transverse (axial) view of the fetal thorax at the level of the four-chamber cardiac view. This is performed by obtaining a true axial plane in which the four cardiac chambers are clearly visualized, with the spine posteriorly and the ribs seen circumferentially, typically just above the diaphragm where the heart occupies approximately one-third of the thoracic area. The outer contour of the chest wall (skin line) is then traced to obtain the circumference measurement.

**PMIDs:** 3555087; 10419608

**Parent term:** Abnormal fetal morphology - HP:0034058; Abnormal fetal skeletal morphology - HP:0025662

### Preferred term label: Abnormal fetal scapula length [READY TO SUBMIT]

**Synonyms:** Abnormal fetal scapula; hypoplastic fetal scapula

**Definition:** An abnormal (short) scapula length is defined as a measurement below the 5th percentile for gestational age or as a value >=3 standard deviations below the mean. The scapula is assessed by measuring the maximal distance from the acromion to the inferior angle. This is performed by first identifying the fetal spine in a longitudinal plane, then sweeping the transducer parallel and laterally, with gentle angulation as needed, until both landmarks are clearly visualized. Digital sonographic calipers are then placed at the acromion and inferior angle to obtain the measurement.

**PMIDs:** 2695411; 7933014

**Parent term:** Abnormal fetal skeletal morphology

**Diseases characterized by this term:** Campomelic dysplasia - OMIM:114290

### Preferred term label: Abnormal fetal lung volume [READY TO SUBMIT]

**Synonyms:** Abnormal fetal FLV

**Definition:** An abnormal fetal lung volume is defined as sonographic and/or fetal MRI evidence that total fetal lung volume is outside the expected range for gestational age, including either decreased lung volume (pulmonary hypoplasia) or increased lung volume (pulmonary enlargement) as determined by specific biometric measurements or other validated imaging criteria.

**Parent term:** Abnormal fetal pulmonary morphology - HP:0034243

**Child terms:** Enlarged fetal lungs (HP:6000613); Decreased fetal lung volume (separate HPO term)

### Preferred term label: Decreased fetal lung volume [READY TO SUBMIT]

**Synonyms:** Decreased fetal FLV

**Definition:** A decreased fetal lung volume is defined as a value below the 5th percentile for gestational age or estimated fetal weight, or >=2 standard deviations below the mean, as measured by three-dimensional (3D) ultrasound or fetal MRI methods, and may be observed across a range of fetal conditions associated with pulmonary hypoplasia, including those that restrict thoracic capacity (e.g. skeletal dysplasias), cause intrathoracic mass effect (e.g. congenital diaphragmatic hernia), reduce amniotic fluid (e.g. Potter sequence or renal dysfunction), impair fetal breathing, or disrupt primary lung development. On ultrasound, fetal lung volume is typically assessed using 3D techniques--either a multiplanar summation-of-areas approach or the rotational VOCAL method--where each lung is measured separately by tracing boundaries across sequential slices, excluding mediastinal structures. When ultrasound assessment is equivocal, fetal MRI may be used to improve prognostication, with lung volume calculated from sequential single-shot fast spin-echo (SSFSE) T2-weighted images in the coronal plane by outlining the lungs across contiguous slices to generate total volume.

**PMIDs:** 25102973; 10419608; 8765272; 20582919; 15459321

**Parent term:** Abnormal fetal lung volume

**Diseases characterized by this term:** Hydrops fetalis (nonimmune, heterogeneous genetic causes) - OMIM 236750; Trisomy 13 - OMIM 613793; Trisomy 18 - OMIM 614748; Myotonic dystrophy type 1 - OMIM 160900; Spinal muscular atrophy - OMIM 253300; Congenital muscular dystrophies (e.g., LAMA2-related) - OMIM 607855; Fetal akinesia deformation sequence (Pena-Shokeir) - OMIM 208150; Anencephaly - OMIM 206500; Bilateral renal agenesis (Potter sequence) - OMIM 191830; ARPKD - OMIM 263200; Congenital diaphragmatic hernia - OMIM 142340, 222400; Thanatophoric dysplasia - OMIM 187600, 187601; Osteogenesis imperfecta type II - OMIM 166210; Achondrogenesis type IA - OMIM 200600; Achondrogenesis type IB - OMIM 600972; Achondrogenesis type II / Hypochondrogenesis - OMIM 200610; Jeune syndrome - OMIM 208500; Campomelic dysplasia - OMIM 114290; SRPS I-IV - OMIM 263530, 263520, 263510, 269860; Platyspondylic lethal skeletal dysplasia, Torrance type - OMIM 151210; Hypophosphatasia (perinatal lethal form) - OMIM 241500; Ellis-van Creveld syndrome - OMIM 225500

## RATIOS

### Preferred term label: Decreased fetal femur length:abdominal circumference ratio [READY TO SUBMIT]

**Synonyms:** Decreased fetal FL:AC ratio

**Definition:** An abnormal (decreased) fetal femur length-to-abdominal circumference (FL/AC) ratio is defined as a value <0.16, which is concerning for a life-limiting skeletal dysplasia. The femur length is measured from the outer border of one end of the ossified femoral diaphysis to the other, excluding the epiphyses and artifact, while the abdominal circumference is obtained in a transverse view by tracing the outer skin line at the level where the stomach, umbilical vein, and portal sinus are visualized. The FL/AC ratio reflects the degree of limb shortening relative to fetal size and has been shown to be one of the best-performing predictors of lethality, outperforming femur length alone and other proportional indices; however, it reflects severity rather than diagnosis.

**PMIDs:** 9369835; 39078648

**Parent term:** Abnormal fetal skeletal morphology - HP:0025662

**Diseases characterized by this term:** Thanatophoric dysplasia type I - OMIM 187600; Thanatophoric dysplasia type II - OMIM 187601; Achondrogenesis type IA - OMIM 200600; Achondrogenesis type IB - OMIM 600972; Achondrogenesis type II / Hypochondrogenesis - OMIM 200610; Osteogenesis imperfecta type II - OMIM 166210; Short-rib thoracic dysplasia (Jeune syndrome) - OMIM 208500; Short-rib polydactyly syndrome types I-IV - OMIM 263530, 263520, 263510, 269860; Campomelic dysplasia - OMIM 114290; Homozygous achondroplasia - OMIM 100800

### Preferred term label: Reduced fetal femur/foot length ratio

Already exists as "Reduced fetal femur/foot length ratio" - https://hpo.jax.org/browse/term/HP:0025663

**Synonyms:** Decreased fetal femur:foot ratio

**Definition:** This ratio relates the length of the fetal femur to that of the fetal foot. The ratio is approximately 1 throughout the age range of 14 to 40 weeks of gestation. A substantial reduction in the ratio can help differentiate fetuses that have dysplastic limb reduction from those whose limbs are short because of constitutional factors or IUGR. It can reduce the problem of an unknown gestational age and help to distinguish between fetal skeletal dysplasia and intrauterine growth retardation caused by other factors.

**Addendum to existing term:** A ratio <0.89 is concerning for a more severe or potentially life-limiting skeletal dysplasia. PMID: 36575575

**PMID:** 36575575

### Preferred term label: Decreased fetal femur length:biparietal diameter ratio [READY TO SUBMIT]

**Synonyms:** Decreased fetal FL:BPD ratio

**Definition:** An abnormal (decreased) fetal femur length-to-biparietal diameter (FL/BPD) ratio is defined as a value <0.376, which is associated with a significantly increased likelihood of skeletal dysplasia. The femur length is measured from the outer border of one end of the ossified femoral diaphysis to the other, excluding the epiphyses and artifact, while the biparietal diameter is measured on ultrasound at the level of the thalami and cavum septi pellucidi, using an outer-to-inner technique (from the outer edge of the proximal skull to the inner edge of the distal skull). This ratio reflects limb shortening relative to head size and serves as a screening marker for skeletal dysplasia.

**PMID:** 36575575

### Preferred term label: Fetal frontal bossing [READY TO SUBMIT]

**Definition:** Fetal frontal bossing is defined as abnormal prominence or protrusion of the frontal bone, assessed on the midsagittal profile view of the fetal face on ultrasound. It is typically identified by subjective evaluation on 2D ultrasound, characterized by an exaggerated convexity or bulging of the frontal contour relative to the expected fetal profile.

**Definition:** Fetal craniosynostosis is defined as the premature fusion of one or more cranial sutures in utero, resulting in abnormal skull growth and head shape. It is assessed on prenatal ultrasound by evaluating the contour of the fetal skull and sutures, with findings that may include abnormal head shape

**PMID:** 35998125

### Preferred term label: Fetal brachycephaly [Ready to Submit]

**Definition:** Fetal brachycephaly is defined as an abnormally rounded or shortened skull shape in which the biparietal diameter (BPD) is disproportionately increased relative to the occipitofrontal diameter (OFD), resulting in an elevated cephalic index (CI). The cephalic index is calculated as (BPD/OFD) x 100 and is assessed on ultrasound in an axial plane of the fetal head at the level of the thalami and cavum septi pellucidi. Brachycephaly is typically defined as a CI >=83-85, with gestational age-specific norms applied. This finding may represent a normal variant but can also be associated with craniosynostosis (particularly bicoronal synostosis), chromosomal abnormalities such as trisomy 21, skeletal dysplasias, or extrinsic compressive forces.

**PMIDs:** 2677864; 11772173

**Parent term:** Fetal craniosynostosis

**Diseases characterized by this term:** Muenke syndrome - OMIM 602849; Saethre-Chotzen syndrome - OMIM 101400

**Nano-attribution:** https://orcid.org/0000-0002-5177-8109

### Preferred term label: Fetal dolichocephaly [Ready to Submit]

**Definition:** Fetal dolichocephaly is defined as an elongated skull shape in which the occipitofrontal diameter (OFD) is disproportionately increased relative to the biparietal diameter (BPD), resulting in a decreased cephalic index (CI <74). The CI is calculated as (BPD/OFD) x 100 on an axial ultrasound view at the level of the thalami and cavum septi pellucidi. It is most commonly due to positional factors with patent sutures and absence of frontal bossing.

**PMID:** 6787895

**Parent term:** Fetal craniosynostosis

**Nano-attribution:** https://orcid.org/0000-0002-5177-8109

### Preferred term label: Fetal scaphocephaly [Ready to Submit]

**Definition:** Fetal scaphocephaly is defined as an elongated, narrow skull shape caused by premature fusion of the sagittal suture (sagittal craniosynostosis). It is characterized by anteroposterior elongation with biparietal narrowing, resulting in a decreased cephalic index, and is assessed on ultrasound in axial and sagittal views of the fetal head. Unlike dolichocephaly, cranial sutures are fused rather than patent, and frontal and occipital bossing are often present and may become more pronounced over time.

**PMIDs:** 28553772; 32868470

**Parent term:** Fetal craniosynostosis

**Diseases characterized by this term:** Thanatophoric dysplasia type II - OMIM 187601; Antley-Bixler syndrome - OMIM 207410

### Preferred term label: Fetal cloverleaf skull [Ready to Submit]

**Synonyms:** Fetal kleeblattschadel

**Definition:** Fetal cloverleaf skull (Kleeblattschadel) is defined as a severe form of multisuture craniosynostosis resulting in a characteristic trilobar skull shape. It is caused by premature fusion of multiple cranial sutures, most commonly the coronal and lambdoid sutures, with compensatory expansion through remaining patent regions. On prenatal ultrasound, it is identified by a distinctive trilobed cranial contour on axial views, with three bulging regions (two temporal and one frontal/superior), often accompanied by turricephaly, abnormal head biometry, ventriculomegaly, and marked proptosis. This finding reflects severe disruption of normal skull growth and is associated with significant morbidity.

**PMID:** 7346817

**Parent term:** Fetal craniosynostosis

**Diseases characterized by this term:** Thanatophoric dysplasia type II - OMIM 187601; Pfeiffer syndrome - OMIM 101600; Crouzon syndrome - OMIM 123500; Apert syndrome - OMIM 101200; Carpenter syndrome - OMIM 201000

### Preferred term label: Abnormal fetal ribs [Ready to submit]

**Definition:** Abnormal fetal ribs are defined as deviations in the number, size, shape, or configuration of the ribs identified on prenatal ultrasound. These abnormalities may include shortened ribs, absent or supernumerary ribs, irregular contour, fractures, or abnormal spacing. The ribs are assessed on axial and coronal views of the fetal thorax, where they should appear as symmetric, evenly spaced echogenic arcs encircling the chest. Abnormal rib findings often reflect underlying skeletal dysplasias or thoracic restriction and may contribute to a small thoracic cage and risk of pulmonary hypoplasia.

**PMID:** 40952954

**Parent term:** Abnormal fetal skeletal morphology - HP:0025662

**Nano-attribution:** https://orcid.org/0000-0002-5177-8109

### Preferred term label: Decreased fetal rib length [Ready to submit]

**Synonyms:** Short fetal ribs

**Definition:** Decreased fetal rib length is defined as ribs that are shorter than expected for gestational age, typically below the 5th percentile or associated with a disproportionately small thorax. It is assessed on ultrasound in axial views of the chest, though surrogate measures such as thoracic circumference are often used. This finding is a key marker of skeletal dysplasia and is associated with pulmonary hypoplasia and increased risk of perinatal lethality.

**PMID:** 8705412

**Parent term:** Abnormal fetal ribs

### Preferred term label: Fetal rib fractures [Ready to submit]

**Synonyms:** Fetal rib beading

**Definition:** Fetal rib fractures are defined as disruptions of the normal continuity of the rib cortex identified on prenatal ultrasound. They appear as discontinuities, angulations, or areas of callus formation along the rib shafts. Rib beading refers to a related finding characterized by a "rosary bead" or "string of pearls" appearance, reflecting multiple healing fractures or callus formation at regular intervals. These findings are most commonly associated with disorders of bone fragility, such as osteogenesis imperfecta.

**PMID:** 3293447

**Parent term:** Abnormal fetal ribs

**Diseases characterized by this term:** Osteogenesis imperfecta (general) - OMIM 166200; Osteogenesis imperfecta type II - OMIM 166210; Achondrogenesis type IA - OMIM 200600; Achondrogenesis type IB - OMIM 600972; Achondrogenesis type II - OMIM 200610

**Definition:** Abnormal fetal spine is defined as any deviation in the alignment, segmentation, curvature, or integrity of the vertebral column identified on prenatal ultrasound. It is assessed in sagittal, coronal, and axial planes, where the spine should appear as a continuous, evenly spaced series of ossification centers with normal curvature.

**Synonyms:** Fetal bone bowing

**Definition:** Long bone bowing is defined as abnormal curvature or angulation of the diaphysis of one or more long bones, most commonly the femur and tibia. It is assessed on prenatal ultrasound in sagittal views, where the long bones should appear straight and symmetric bilaterally. ### Preferred term label: Fetal long bone epiphyseal abnormality [Ready to submit]

**Synonyms:** Abnormal fetal epiphyses

**Definition:** Fetal long bone epiphyseal abnormality is defined as abnormal size, shape, timing, or ossification of the secondary ossification centers at the ends of long bones. On ultrasound, these epiphyses normally appear as echogenic, egg-shaped foci in a gestational age-dependent sequence but may be delayed, absent, irregular, or enlarged when abnormal. These findings may indicate skeletal dysplasia or disordered endochondral ossification.

**PMID:** 6726877

### Preferred term label: Fetal long bone metaphyseal abnormality [Ready to submit]

**Synonyms:** Abnormal fetal metaphyses

**Definition:** Fetal long bone metaphyseal abnormality is defined as abnormal size, shape, contour, or mineralization of the metaphysis, the region between the diaphysis and epiphysis of a long bone. On ultrasound, the metaphysis may appear flared, widened, irregular, cupped, or poorly ossified. These findings reflect abnormal endochondral bone formation and are commonly associated with skeletal dysplasias.
